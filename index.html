Good to know

Plastering questions, answered

How long does a plastering job usually take?
Most single rooms are skimmed in a day, and ceilings or small repairs are often quicker. Larger jobs — full re-boards, whole-house work or external rendering — run over several days. George gives you a clear timescale with your quote, so you know exactly what to plan around.
How long does fresh plaster take to dry before I can paint?
Fresh plaster goes from dark brown to an even pale pink as it dries, which usually takes anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks depending on the room, the weather and how well it's ventilated. Wait until it's fully dry and one even colour, then seal it with a watered-down mist coat before your top coats of paint.
Do you do external rendering as well as internal plastering?
Yes — alongside interior plastering, George does external rendering, including modern silicone and through-coloured renders built to stand up to Devon's wet, coastal weather. That covers full house renders, conservatories and patch repairs to existing render.
Do you do emergency repairs after a leak or water damage?
Yes. Water-damaged or collapsed ceilings after a leak, burst pipe or slipped roof tile are some of the most common urgent jobs George gets called out for. He'll re-board and re-skim the damage and blend it into the rest of the room — send a photo on WhatsApp and he'll tell you how quickly he can get to you.
What areas do you cover?
George covers Exeter, Crediton, Tiverton, Exmouth, Newton Abbot, Okehampton, Cullompton and the surrounding towns and villages right across Devon. If you're not sure whether you're in range, just ask.
Can you skim over artex or old, uneven walls?
In most cases, yes. Artexed or uneven ceilings and walls can either be skimmed straight over once they're properly prepared, or boarded out first for a perfectly flat finish — George will advise which is right for your wall when he quotes.
Can you plaster over wallpaper?
It's best not to. Plaster needs a clean, solid surface to grip, and the moisture in fresh plaster can loosen old wallpaper paste — which pulls the new plaster away and ruins the finish. George strips the walls back and preps them properly first, so you get a smooth, long-lasting result rather than a quick fix that fails.
Will plastering make a lot of mess, and do you clean up afterwards?
Plastering is wet work, but it doesn't have to wreck your home. George puts dust sheets down to protect floors and anything left in the room, works clean, and tidies up properly at the end — so you're left with fresh walls, not a mess to sort out.
Do I need to clear the room before you start?
It helps if the room is as clear as possible — the more space to work, the faster and cleaner the job. If big furniture can't be moved, George will work around it and sheet it up. Just let him know what's staying when he quotes so he can plan for it.
