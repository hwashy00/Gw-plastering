Plastering in Exmouth

Exmouth and the surrounding coast are a regular run for George — and a stretch where good rendering really earns its keep.

Living by the sea looks lovely; it's harder on a building. Salt-laden air and driving rain off the estuary chew through tired render and find their way into any crack. A lot of George's work around Exmouth is external — re-rendering seafront terraces and bungalows, sorting weather damage, and getting walls properly sealed against the elements so the inside stays dry. It's worth doing right the first time on the coast.

Inside, it's the usual mix — skimming tired rooms, taking down old artex ceilings, re-boarding after a leak, and patching up before a sale or a let. Whether it's a Victorian terrace near the front or a newer bungalow out toward Littleham, the standard's the same: prepped properly, finished smooth, left clean.
