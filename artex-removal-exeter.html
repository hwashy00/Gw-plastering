Goodbye swirly ceilings

Those textured artex ceilings instantly date a room — here's how George brings them back to a smooth, modern finish.

Artex — the swirled, stippled or combed textured finish on so many ceilings and walls — was everywhere in UK homes from the 1970s through the 1990s. It does one thing brilliantly: make a room feel dated. The good news is it's very fixable, and there are two clean ways to deal with it.

If the artex is sound and not too heavy, George can usually skim straight over it to a smooth, flat finish. If it's badly uneven, damaged, or coming away, the better route is to overboard it — fix fresh plasterboard over the top and skim that — which guarantees a perfectly flat result. George will look at your ceiling and tell you which is right rather than reaching for whichever is quickest.

A word on asbestos — handled properly

This matters, so George is straight about it: artex applied before around the year 2000 can contain a small amount of asbestos. Left alone or skimmed over, it's not a risk — the danger only comes from disturbing it, particularly by dry-sanding or scraping, which releases fibres into the air.

That's exactly why George's default approach is to skim over or overboard old artex rather than aggressively sanding it off — it's the safe, clean way to get a smooth finish. If your artex is from before 2000 and you're unsure, it's worth getting a quick asbestos test first; if it does contain asbestos and needs taking off rather than covering, that's a job for a licensed asbestos removal specialist, and George will tell you so honestly rather than risk it.

Plastering questions

Does my artex contain asbestos?
It might, if it was applied before around the year 2000 — older artex sometimes contains a small amount of asbestos. It's harmless left undisturbed, but if you're unsure and it needs removing rather than covering, it's worth a quick asbestos test first. George skims over or boards rather than dry-sanding, which keeps things safe.
Can you skim over artex, or does it have to come off?
In many cases it can be skimmed straight over once it's prepared — which is cleaner and safer than removing it. If the texture is very heavy or uneven, boarding over it gives a better, flatter result. George will advise which suits your ceiling.
Is artex removal messy?
Skimming over or overboarding is far cleaner than scraping and sanding. George sheets everything up and works tidily, so you're left with a smooth ceiling rather than a dust-covered room.
How long does an artex ceiling take?
A typical room ceiling is usually a day or two depending on size and whether it's skimmed over or boarded first. George will give you a clear timescale with your quote.
