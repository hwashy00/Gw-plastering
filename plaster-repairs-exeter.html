Not every job needs a full re-plaster

Plenty of plastering is repair work — putting right cracks, blown patches and water damage so they vanish into the wall.

A lot of what George does isn't full rooms — it's repairs. A crack that's appeared, a patch of plaster that's blown and sounds hollow, a hole where a radiator or fitting came off, or a ceiling stained and sagging after a leak. These don't need the whole wall doing — just a tidy, well-matched repair that blends invisibly into the surrounding plaster so you'd never know it was there.

The most common urgent one in Devon homes is water damage — a leaking roof, a burst pipe or an overflow leaving a brown-stained, blown or sagging ceiling. That needs sorting before it spreads, and George can re-board and re-skim the damaged area and make good around it. The trick with any repair is matching the finish so the patch disappears, rather than leaving an obvious flat square on a textured wall.

Water damage & emergencies

Water damage is the one that usually can't wait. A leak from a bathroom, a slipped roof tile or a burst pipe can leave a ceiling stained, sagging or blown — and the longer it's left, the further it spreads and the bigger the job becomes. George prioritises these: re-boarding and re-skimming the damaged area, then blending the repair into the rest of the room so it's invisible once painted.

Plastering questions

My ceiling's water-damaged after a leak — can you fix it?
Yes — that's one of the most common urgent jobs George gets called for. Once the leak itself is stopped, he re-boards and re-skims the damaged area and blends it into the rest of the ceiling so it's invisible once painted.
Can you repair a crack without re-plastering the whole wall?
Usually, yes. Most cracks and blown patches can be cut out, filled and skimmed locally and matched to the surrounding wall — no need to do the whole room. George will tell you honestly if a wall genuinely needs more than a patch.
Do you do emergency repairs?
George prioritises urgent jobs like water-damaged or collapsed ceilings. Send a photo on WhatsApp and he'll tell you how quickly he can get to you — there's no call-out charge to come and look.
Why does plaster crack or blow?
Common causes are building settlement, vibration, leaks, or — in older homes — old lath-and-plaster losing its grip over time. The repair approach depends on the cause, which is why George looks before quoting.
